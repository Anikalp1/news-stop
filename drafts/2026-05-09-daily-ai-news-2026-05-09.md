# Data Platforms Rise, Healthcare AI Bets, and Layoff Reality Check

Telefónica is building data infrastructure for AI ecosystems while healthcare leaders call for smarter AI investments. Meanwhile, the debate over data access intensifies and Gartner warns that AI-driven layoffs may backfire.

## Telefónica Launches Data Spaces Platform for Artificial Intelligence Ecosystems

**What happened:**  
Telefónica has launched a Data Spaces Platform designed to support artificial intelligence ecosystems.

**Why it matters:**  
Developers building AI applications now have a new platform option for managing data spaces, potentially simplifying how teams share and access training data across organizations.

## Op-ed: It's time to make more strategic bets on AI in healthcare

**What happened:**  
An op-ed argues that healthcare needs more strategic investments in artificial intelligence.

**Why it matters:**  
Healthcare developers and startups have a clear signal that thoughtful, targeted AI solutions will win over generic automation tools.

## AI wants direct access to your data

**What happened:**  
A blog post explores how AI systems increasingly seek direct data access, with commentary on Hacker News.

**Why it matters:**  
Developers building AI-powered applications need to consider data access patterns and user privacy trade-offs when designing system architectures.

## Gartner: AI layoffs don't create returns, they just create vacancies

**What happened:**  
Gartner reports that cutting staff due to AI adoption doesn't generate expected returns and instead leaves companies understaffed.

**Why it matters:**  
Engineering leaders should focus on AI augmentation rather than replacement—your team's expertise remains essential for deploying and maintaining AI systems effectively.
