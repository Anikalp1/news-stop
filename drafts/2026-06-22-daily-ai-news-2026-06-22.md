# AI Builds Games, Eyes Elections, and Drones Go Software

AI is popping up in unexpected corners today—from a kid-powered voxel game to election strategies, while developers gain tools for secure AI access and code quality. Here's what's shaping the field.

## Show HN: A 100% AI-generated 3D voxel game I built with my 10yo son

**What happened:**  
A developer created a 3D voxel game entirely with AI, collaborating with their 10-year-old child. The project is hosted on GitHub.

**Why it matters:**  
It shows how AI tools are lowering barriers for creative coding, enabling non-experts to build complex projects. For developers, it’s a reminder that AI can accelerate prototyping and inspire hands-on learning.

## AI and tech are trying to influence the midterm elections

**What happened:**  
NPR reports on AI and tech efforts to sway midterm elections, sparking discussion on ethical boundaries.

**Why it matters:**  
Developers should stay alert to AI’s political applications and their risks. Tools and frameworks that prioritize transparency and accountability will be critical as AI intersects with voting systems and public opinion.

## Local lint, type-check and AI security dashboard for modern full-stack projects

**What happened:**  
ProjectLens, an npm package, adds AI-powered linting, type-checking, and security dashboards for full-stack projects.

**Why it matters:**  
It streamlines code quality and security checks, blending traditional tooling with AI insights. Useful for developers juggling modern stacks and safety concerns.

## The OpenSSL Library AI Policy

**What happened:**  
OpenSSL Library published its AI policy, outlining principles for AI integration in security-critical software.

**Why it matters:**  
Developers relying on OpenSSL should review how AI features align with security standards. The policy signals growing institutional attention to AI’s role in foundational tools.

## The Indian Drone Industry’s Shift Away From Manufacturing to Software - Analytics India Magazine
