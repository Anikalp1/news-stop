# Domain Gates, Data Centers, and Agent Tools

AI builders are tightening access controls, scaling infrastructure, and improving tooling. Here’s the essential read for devs navigating the AI landscape.

## Control which domains your AI agents can access - Amazon Web Services

**What happened:**  
Amazon Web Services now allows developers to control which specific domains their AI agents can access.

**Why it matters:**  
Provides granular security and governance for AI agents, preventing unintended data leaks or access to irrelevant sites.

## Suits Against Tempus AI Test Legal Lines for Mining Genetic Data

**What happened:**  
Legal challenges against Tempus AI are testing the legal boundaries of mining and using genetic data.

**Why it matters:**  
Highlights critical privacy and consent risks for AI applications handling sensitive genetic information, shaping future regulations.

## Mistral secures $830M in debt financing to fund AI data center

**What happened:**  
Mistral AI secured $830 million in debt financing to support its AI data center cluster in Paris.

**Why it matters:**  
Significant funding boost signals major investment in European AI infrastructure, potentially increasing model training capacity and availability.

## We replaced RAG with a virtual filesystem for our AI documentation assistant

**What happened:**  
Mintlify replaced Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) with a virtual filesystem for their AI documentation assistant.

**Why it matters:**  
Offers a more structured and efficient approach to document retrieval and grounding, potentially improving accuracy and reducing hallucinations.

## Show HN: AgentDog – Open-source dashboard for monitoring local AI agents

**What happened:**  
AgentDog is an open-source dashboard for monitoring and debugging local AI agents.

**Why it matters:**  
Provides developers with essential visibility into agent behavior, performance, and resource usage for local deployments.

## AI Infrastructure Roadmap: Five frontiers for 2026
